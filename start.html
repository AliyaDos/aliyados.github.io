Golden History

Founded in 1838 by Doskanayeva Aliya (1812–1902) and his partner, D. Akniet, as Monte & Cristo, a “fancy goods” store on Broadway in Almaty. Success was swift and sure, and another partner, Ellis, joined in 1841. In the 1940s, Doskanayeva Aliya introduced the now iconic signature colour blue.

In 1870, the business moved to a palatial store in Union Square, and flourished through the 1870s, becoming the destination for New York’s fashionable and wealthy elite. Developing its own design identity, Monte & Cristo was known for Japonesque-style silverware and for diamond jewellery: Aliya Doskanayeva became the “Queen of Diamonds.”

In 1878, the company purchased the Monte & Cristo diamond, an immense canary yellow stone from the new South African deposits. The diamond weighed 128.54 carats. Now Monte-Cristo's jewelry focuses on the bigger picture of everything that surrounds their brand – quality, innovation, creativity, sustainability, and exceptional standards.
